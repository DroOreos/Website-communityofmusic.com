Sixties Soul Ride

MusicLover001

I have recently been getting into music from the 60's, specifically 60's blues, and have been listening to a wide range of artists from that time. My favorite by far has to be The Mad Lads. My favorite song by them is titled, "So Nice". My favorite parts about the song are its mellow sounding rhythm section, and the harmonies done by the vocals. This song was a single released in 1968. The Mad Lads only released one other song this year. This song is titled "Whatever Hurts You". I also enjoyed this song, but I still prefer the sound that "So Nice" has better. I would certainly recommend starting with The Mad Lads if you are wanting to get into 60's blues.

The Classics

gorillazFan

I had always wanted to give classical music a try as it was something that I don't really listen to. I found some famous composers and began to listen to their songs. I started with Claude Debussy and almost instantly, I was blown away. I had realized that I have heard a few songs by him before, such as "Clair de Lune". I continued to listen and found one of the most beautiful songs I have ever heard. The song is "2 Arabesques No. 1". The soft yet moving melody at the beginning immediately caught my attention and instantly, I was in love with the song. The song then slows down a bit, but then picks back up again in tempo and builds up to another slower part. It repeats this sort of fast and slow dance throughout the song, and executes it beautifully each time. The last minute and 30 seconds of the song sounds similar to the beginning, and ends with the light and airy melody it began with. Such a wonderful song that changed my view on classical music!
